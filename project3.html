PLANT SENTIENCE

A PROTOTYPE TO GIVE PLANTS A VOICE

  • MEDIUM:


    PHYSICAL COMPUTING

  • TIMEFRAME:


    NOVEMBER 2022

  • SETTING:


    PARSONS SCHOOL OF DESIGN


A common issue among plant owners is how one day, a plant can look completely fine, and then die after a few days. This is because when the plant is overwatered, experiences a cold draft, or lives in low humidity, they can't communicate that they're suffering right away.


The way to resolve that issue would just be to monitor the plant all the time like what houseplant mass producers do. Instead of having a straightforward solution, the project is meant to be more interpretive and give these plants a voice. This was done by altering a glass plant jar. Sensors were attached either on top or in the direction of the nearest window, and then connected to Arduino.




A pitch and octave are assigned to different ranges of temperatures, humidity, and light levels. Good levels follow a key, which makes the music sound pleasant.




Bad levels do not follow the key, which makes the music sound bad, or at least different. If the plant could have better conditions, the music would sound incomplete or a bit strange.




For future iterations, reverb can be added to the notes to be more pleasing, and perhaps the plants could talk to each other for support, much like how plants when grouped together, have better humidity.
